By D and M on April 29, 2016
Color: Copper
This is a sturdy well constructed copper mug. This mug is made of 100% copper as far as I can tell. I took a strong magnet to it and there is definitely no steel. There is a nice finish on the mug. It is a lacquer type finish that gives it a more worn antique look to it. The lip of the mug is comfortable to drink from. I had a problem with the lip on the mug from a different manufacturer. This does not have that same problem.

There is one thing in the description that I don't think is quite accurate. Description states "the thermal conductivity of the copper keeps your drink colder for longer periods of time." This is not true. Copper is conductive so it will conduct heat from the air into the drink making it not stay cold as long as an insulated mug. The one thing the copper does though, again because of its conductivity, is make the drink feel cold to your lips. When you put your lips to the cold mug the copper will make your lips cold and therefore making the drink feel colder.

One of the things that I like about a copper mug is the possible antimicrobial properties this mug provides. Copper produces an oligodynamic effect on algae, molds, spores, fungi, viruses, prokaryotic and eukaryotic microorganisms.
